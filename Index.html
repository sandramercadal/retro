Early Days: The Brick Era (1980's-1990's)

Mobile phones first hit the big screen in the 1980's, quickly becoming symbols of wealth and power with their bulky "brick" designs. These devices were notoriously large. In the 1987 film "Wall Street," Michael Douglas epitomized this trend, exuding cool confidence as he talked on his oversized mobile phone.

The Flip Phone Revolution (1990's-2000's)

The 1990's-2000's marked the rise of the flip phone — sleek, compact, and synonymous with teen culture, fashion and style. From Cher’s iconic flip phone in "Clueless" to Keanu Reeves’ distinctive banana phone in "The Matrix", the flip phone became a statement of style and a symbol of technological progress.
